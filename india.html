National parks in India

India has varied landscapes, from towering mountain ranges to massive deserts, pristine beaches and lush forests. It is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, making it a supreme destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. One of the greatest ways to experience India's natural beauty is by visiting its national parks in India.
India is home to a diverse array of national parks, protecting its rich biodiversity. Some notable ones include Jim Corbett National Park, known for its tigers; Kaziranga National Park, a haven for one-horned rhinoceros; and Ranthambore National Park, famous for its majestic wildlife and historic ruins. These parks showcase India's natural beauty and conservation efforts.

A Look at India's Amazing Linguistic Diversity

India is a heterogeneous country, in all aspects of the word heterogeneous. The land is an interesting amalgamation of varied history, traditions, religions and languages. With the passage of time and the requirements of the natives, the country evolved drastically making way for underlying languages.Each language reflects the unique history, traditions and customs of the people who speak it, making India a melting pot of linguistic and cultural diversity. Approximately, 19, 500 languages (tribal and non-tribal) are spoken in this country. Out of which, certain prominent languages of India are heard the most.
Language plays a crucial role in the creation of culture. It is through language that individuals are able to represent and express their world, including the identities, groups, and categories that make up social life.
